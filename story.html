I made Sudoku feel like Mahjong

Mahjong Sudoku Classic is a calm puzzle game built around a small idea: keep the clean logic of Sudoku, but change the feeling by replacing numbers with Mahjong-inspired tile suits.

The idea

Sudoku is already one of the clearest puzzle formats: every row, column, and 3x3 area needs one of each symbol. Most versions use numbers because numbers are universal and easy to scan.

I wanted to keep that logic, but make the board feel a little warmer and more cultural. Mahjong tiles have a shape, rhythm, and visual personality that numbers do not have. They also naturally come in suits, which makes them a good fit for a puzzle that is about patterns.

How it plays

The rules are still classic Sudoku. You solve each row, column, and 3x3 area using one of each tile from 1 to 9. The difference is the visual language: instead of staring at a plain number grid, you can choose Mahjong-style suits such as dots, bamboo, or characters.

That makes the puzzle familiar enough for Sudoku players, but different enough to feel fresh.

Why I built it

I like small games that can be understood quickly and played quietly. Mahjong Sudoku Classic is not trying to be noisy or complicated. It is meant to be a calm puzzle that you can open for a few minutes, solve at your own pace, and close without any account or heavy setup.

It also became a small bridge between two puzzle traditions: the strict logic of Sudoku and the tile identity of Mahjong.

What I want feedback on

I am especially interested in feedback from puzzle players:

  • Do Mahjong-style symbols make Sudoku more enjoyable, or harder to scan?
  • Which tile suit feels easiest to play: dots, bamboo, or characters?
  • Should future versions include more visual themes or stay very minimal?
  • Would you prefer daily puzzles, difficulty levels, or a calmer free-play mode?
